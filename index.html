How to Choose the Right Gold Detector

Frequently Asked Questions

What's the difference between VLF and PI detectors? +

VLF (Very Low Frequency): Best for beginners, good for small gold, less expensive, good ground balancing.

PI (Pulse Induction): Better for deep gold, works in mineralized soil, more expensive, steeper learning curve.

How deep can metal detectors detect gold? +

Detection depth depends on several factors: gold size, soil type, detector type, and coil size. Small flakes: 2-4 inches. Small nuggets: 6-12 inches. Large nuggets: 12-24+ inches.

Is waterproof rating important? +

Yes! If you plan to detect in wet areas, streams, or beaches, waterproof rating is essential. Look for at least IP67 rating for water resistance.

How much does a good metal detector cost? +

Budget options: $100-$300. Mid-range: $300-$800. Professional: $800-$2000+. Start with a budget option to learn, then upgrade if needed.

What's the best location to find gold? +

Look for areas with historical gold mining activity, near streams and riverbeds, in rocky areas, and on public lands. Always check local regulations and get permission before detecting.

Do I need a license to metal detect? +

Requirements vary by location. Some areas require permits, others don't. Always check local laws, get permission from landowners, and follow all regulations in your area.
